    Credit Repair

    At Capital Correct we go over your credit with a fine tooth come to get a full picture of your score. We then make a plan of corrective actions that we can take to increase your score. As well as inform you on ways to keep the increase once completed. Here are some tips below.

    • Check Your Credit Report Regularly:

      • Obtain a copy of your credit report from each of the major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion).
      • Review your report for inaccuracies, discrepancies, or fraudulent activities.
      • Dispute any errors you find with the respective credit bureau.

    • Pay Your Bills on Time

      • On-time payments have a significant impact on your credit score.
      • Set up automatic payments or reminders to ensure you pay bills by the due date.
      • Even one late payment can have a negative effect, so make punctual payments a priority.We work to get items removed.

    • Reduce Credit Card Balances

      • Aim to keep your credit card balances well below your credit limit.
      • High credit utilization (the ratio of credit card balances to credit limits) can negatively impact your credit score.
      • Pay down existing balances and try to keep your utilization rate below 30%.

    • Establish a Mix of Credit Types

      • Having a mix of credit types, such as credit cards, installment loans, and retail accounts, can positively influence your credit score.
      • However, only open new credit accounts when necessary, and avoid opening several accounts within a short period.

    • Avoid Closing Old Credit Accounts

      • The length of your credit history is a factor in your credit score.
      • Keep older, positive accounts open, as they contribute to a longer credit history, which can be beneficial.

    • Be Cautious with New Credit Applications

      • Each time you apply for new credit, a hard inquiry is made on your credit report, which can temporarily lower your score.There are some exceptions.
      • Limit the number of new credit applications, especially within a short time frame.

    • Address Delinquent Accounts

      • If you have any accounts in collections or that are past due, work on bringing them current.We help you deal with creditors.
      • Negotiate with creditors to set up payment plans or settlements if needed.

    • Seek Professional Help if Necessary

      • If your credit situation is complex or you're struggling to make progress, consider a consultation with Capital Correct.
    Remember, improving your credit score is a gradual process, and it's important to stay patient and committed to positive financial habits over time.

    Succession Planning

    Selling a business is a significant decision that should be carefully considered. We know life happens and you may feel it time for a change. We provide succession planning for all types of situations.Here are five common reasons why entrepreneurs and business owners may choose to sell their businesses:

    • Retirement
      • Business owners often decide to sell when they are ready to retire. Selling the business allows them to convert the value of their business into liquid assets that can be used to fund their retirement lifestyle or pursue other interests.
    • Financial Gain
      • One of the primary reasons for selling a business is the opportunity to realize a significant financial gain. The owner may have received a lucrative offer that provides a substantial return on their investment and years of hard work.
    • Burnout or Lifestyle Change
      • Entrepreneurs may choose to sell their businesses due to burnout or a desire for a change in lifestyle. The demands of running a business can be intense, and some owners may decide to sell to reduce stress, spend more time with family, or pursue other personal interests.
    • Strategic Shift or Focus
      • Businesses may be sold as part of a strategic decision to shift focus or reallocate resources. This could involve selling a non-core business unit to streamline operations, exit an unprofitable market, or concentrate on a more promising venture.
    • Market Conditions or Industry Changes
      • External factors such as changes in market conditions or industry dynamics may influence a decision to sell. For example, a business may face increased competition, regulatory challenges, or technological disruptions that make selling a viable option for mitigating risks or capitalizing on market trends.
